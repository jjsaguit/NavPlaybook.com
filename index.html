Holistic Evangelizing


Essentials of Holistic Evangelizing

Five Parts of Evangelism Process

  1. Initiating/Building Relationships

  2. Transitioning from casual conversation to spiritual conversation

  3. Proclamation of Holistic Gospel

  4. Ushering them to the Kingdom

  5. Demonstrating the Gospel – all the parts of evangelism.

Three Parts of the Holistic Gospel

  • The Bad News about Sin
    • Sin Caused the Man’s broken relationship with God, himself, others and Environment.

  • The Good News about Jesus
    • The life, death and resurrection of Jesus has restored everything that Sin has destroyed. Relationship with God, self, other and the environment, It’s fullness is coming.

  • The Right response to the Gospel
    • Repent – turn away from ways sin and turn to the ways of Jesus
    • Believing – trust what Jesus did as the only way to be saved.
    • Accept – Jesus as the only savior and Lord of our lives.

Holistic Establishing


Essentials of Holistic Establishing

Curriculum Outline: Essentials of Discipleship

  1. Identity with Christ – Loved and assured by God in Jesus. Child of God.

  2. Communication with God – Loving Conversation with God though prayer and God talking to him through the Word.

  3. Trust – Faith in God. He can be trusted with everything in life.

  4. Lordship – Will obey Jesus because He is trust worthy, and His ways is best.

  5. Stewardship – God is the owner of everything we have. We are His steward.

  6. Relationships – Loving towards our brethren, compassionate to the Lost.

  7. Basic Doctrines

Connecting Real Life Situation to the Curriculum

  • Parenting
    • Help develop close relationship with God, despite any situation in life they are in.
    • Trust God for all the situation in life.
    • See how God will work is our situation.

  • Counselling
    • Help when encountering hang ups and hindrances for growth and relationships.
    • Point him back to Jesus and our identity with Christ.

  • Discipling
    • Encourage to obey Jesus in his situation whatever the cost.
    • Help him recognize the Lordship of Jesus in His situation and to whatever God entrusted to him.

The Process



The Process:

  • Evangelizing – Telling the Lost about the Good News about Jesus.

  • Establishing – Helping Believers to grow in relationship with Jesus and follow Him.

  • Equipping – Helping the Disciples for the work of the harvest .

  • Engaging – Sending Laborers into the harvest

The People:

  • Lost – People who have not trusted Jesus as their Personal Lord and Savior.

  • Believers (Navs)– Are those who have trusted Jesus as Lord and Savior but are still immature.

  • Disciples (Navs)– Are those who are growing as committed followers of Jesus.

  • Laborers - are those who gets involve in the harvest

SBC



Small Biblical Community (SBC)

  • The Bible Study groups compose of 3-10 people.

  • They have common purpose as small community.

  • They meet at least weekly for:
    • Study of the Word together to listen to God and respond to Him in prayer.
    • Express their love, care and concerns and see how they can serve one another.
    • Pray, plan and prepare on how they can advance the Kingdom of God together.
    • One on One sessions to help individuals are also regular.

  • SBC is the cutting edge of our Ministry.
    • This where the foundation of discipleship is happening.
    • This is where the movement of the Gospel of God’s Kingdom is also happening.

1O1



One on One (1O1)

  • A session between the leader and person he is helping.

  • The purposes include:
    • Coaching – helping someone achieve his goals.
    • Mentoring – passing on the vision, skills, conviction, knowledge and position to someone.
    • Counselling – Helping someone to untangle from his present hang-ups and struggles.
    • Discipling – Helping someone to follow and obey Jesus.
    • Encouraging/exulting someone to God in his present situation.
    • Deepening friendships
    • Journeying together.

  • This is happening among the SBC’s and Teams, and on special situation

Roles


Role of FS, IS, YDF, AS and NA: The Four Contribution

  • Mobile Pioneers (Full time Staff: FS, IS)
    • Pioneering a ministry (New entity or new places) with in the ministry area, region and mission fields, by team or individuals.

  • Local Laborers - SBC leaders (NA and YDF)
    • Are those who are leading small groups (SBC) by team or individuals.

  • Local Leaders - ELC and PLC Leaders, RC (AS)
    • Are those who are leading an Entity, ministry area or region either by team or individuals

  • Mobile Alongsider (Staff: FS, Role: ET, ED, Overseers, RC, Area Leaders)
    • Those are the people who go around helping SBC, ELC and PLC leaders in terms of focus, capacity building and coaching.
    • Helping leaders in their struggles, hindrances and other challenges both personal and ministry.
    • Help Leaders and laborers on their personal development.
